Research: "Developing a Used Motorcycle Price Prediction Model Using Linear Regression and Random Forest Algorithms"

Abstract: This research discusses how JUV MOTORSPORT, a used motorcycle dealer in the JABODETABEK area, uses Linear Regression and Random Forest algorithms to predict used motorcycle prices. The purpose of this research is to create an accurate price prediction model, which helps the dealer determine a competitive selling price, while increasing the company's profitability.

1. Background

JUV MOTORSPORT faces challenges in determining competitive and accurate used motorcycle selling prices. The lack of a reliable prediction model negatively impacts the company's sales and profit margins. A data-driven approach is needed to solve this inefficiency.

2. Methodology

This research uses the CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) process, which includes the stages of:

  • Business Understanding
  • Data Understanding
  • Data Preparation
  • Modeling & Evaluation

The data is taken from the public dataset “BIKE_DATASET” on Kaggle, utilizing attributes such as price, mileage, weight, and motor type.

3. Models and Analysis

  • Linear Regression: Used to understand the relationship between price and other variables. The model has an adjusted R-square of 0.6051, indicating a significant relationship, though data outliers slightly reduce its accuracy.
  • Random Forest: This model shows a lower ASE (Average Squared Error) value, indicating more accurate and robust predictions compared to Linear Regression.

4. Evaluation Results

The Random Forest model performed better in terms of ASE and SSE (Sum of Squared Errors), making it more suitable for predicting used motorcycle prices. The model evaluation also highlighted that the mileage and weight of the motorcycle are the main factors influencing the final price.

5. Implementation and Recommendations

After evaluation, the Random Forest model was implemented as an API accessible by the JUV MOTORSPORT system. Future recommendations include further feature engineering, hyperparameter tuning, and cross-validation to enhance the model's precision.
